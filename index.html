How We Work

Operational discipline, without the overhead.

Every engagement at Low Tide Ops is built around three operating principles that distinguish institutional project discipline from informal project management.

01
We organize what you already have.
We don't introduce new systems or require new behaviors from your team. We take the information your project already generates — through existing tools, calls, and site visits — and organize it into professional, consistent deliverables. The builder or developer stays in their workflow. We layer the controls infrastructure on top.
02
Nothing goes out without your approval.
Every owner-facing or stakeholder-facing deliverable is reviewed and approved by the builder or client before it is published or distributed. This is a core operating principle, not a courtesy. You control the narrative of your project. We provide the system that makes it professional.
03
We are selective by design.
Low Tide Ops takes on a limited number of engagements at any time. This is not a scalability constraint — it is a deliberate quality decision. Every client gets focused, senior-level attention. We would rather work with three projects exceptionally than ten projects adequately.
About

Institutional background.
Boutique focus.

Low Tide Ops was founded by Michael Brown, a development manager with direct experience overseeing project controls for institutional real estate portfolios. Michael has spent his career at the intersection of real estate development and operational discipline — managing budgets, schedules, consultant teams, and stakeholder reporting on projects where execution quality is not optional.

The problem Low Tide Ops addresses is one he observed repeatedly: boutique builders and developers do exceptional work, but they rarely have the reporting and controls infrastructure that makes that work legible to the owners, investors, and boards who funded it. That gap creates friction, disputes, and lost referrals that have nothing to do with the quality of the project itself.

Low Tide Ops was built to close that gap — to make institutional-grade project discipline available to the boutique projects and operators that need it and have historically had no good way to access it.
